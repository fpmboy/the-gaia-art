About project

Acrylic paintings form a waterproof, protective layer on exposed tree wood. An experiment with a tree revealed that after four years, a painted area had only two new bark beetle entrances and no signs of wood rotting, while an unpainted area was severely damaged by bark beetles and decay. These exposed parts are like wounds, and acrylic paint acts as an artificial bark, shielding the tree’s inner layers and extending its lifespan.
